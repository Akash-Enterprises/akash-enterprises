Packaging & Logistics Essentials

Packaging and logistics materials are critical to protecting goods during storage, handling, and transportation. Proper selection reduces damage, improves stacking efficiency, and ensures safe movement across supply chains.

How to Choose the Right Packaging Material

  • Assess the product’s weight, fragility, and sensitivity to impact or moisture.
  • Select cushioning, wrapping, or strapping based on handling frequency and transit distance.
  • Match film thickness, tape strength, and sack capacity to load requirements.
  • Consider storage conditions such as humidity, dust, and exposure duration.

Common Buyer Mistakes

  • Using insufficient cushioning for fragile or high-value goods.
  • Over-stretching or under-wrapping loads, leading to instability during transport.
  • Choosing tapes or strapping without checking adhesive or tensile strength.
  • Ignoring waste handling and disposal needs during packaging planning.

Primary Use Cases

  • Warehousing, storage, and pallet protection
  • Domestic and industrial goods transportation
  • Manufacturing dispatch and export packaging
  • Retail, e-commerce, and bulk material handling
